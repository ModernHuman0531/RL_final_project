% Abstract
% ----------------------------------
  \begin{block}{Abstract}
Traffic signal control in urban environments requires balancing vehicle
throughput and pedestrian safety. To address this challenge, we propose an
\textbf{Action-Constrained Proximal Policy Optimisation (PPO)} framework that
integrates pedestrian safety requirements through feasible-action masking
during policy execution. The proposed method is compared with five widely used
traffic-control baselines --- Fixed-Time, Max Pressure, SOTL, DQN-AR, and
SPRe+ --- in both a single-intersection task and a 3$\times$3 grid network
simulated in SUMO. Through these experiments, we examine the potential of
safe and explainable deep reinforcement learning for trustworthy traffic
signal control in smart-city environments.

% ----------------------------------
% Research Objectives
% ----------------------------------
  \begin{alertblock}{Research Objectives}
    \begin{itemize}
      \item \textbf{Objective 1:} Design an action-constrained RL agent that
        enforces pedestrian safety constraints \emph{by construction},
        guaranteeing zero violations without relying on reward shaping alone.
      \item \textbf{Objective 2:} Benchmark the proposed agent against
        rule-based baselines (Fixed-Time, Max Pressure, SOTL) and
        action-constrained learned agents (DQN-AR, SPRe+) on efficiency and
        safety metrics across two network scales.
      \item \textbf{Objective 3:} Validate the computational advantage of
        acceptance-rejection masking over QP-projection (SPRe+) in growing
        action spaces.
    \end{itemize}
  \end{alertblock}

% Methodology
% ----------------------------------
  \begin{block}{Methodology}

    The agent operates within an \textbf{Action-Constrained MDP}. At every
    step, the constraint checker computes the feasible action set
    $\mathcal{C}(s)$ by removing phases that violate any hard constraint. PPO
    selects actions via a \textbf{masked softmax} restricted to $\mathcal{C}(s)$,
    so infeasible actions receive zero probability and can never be executed.

% ----------------------------------
% Agents & Baselines
% ----------------------------------
  \begin{block}{Agents \& Baselines}
    \begin{itemize}
      \item \textbf{Fixed-Time:} Fixed 30\,s NS/EW cycle. No traffic awareness.
      \item \textbf{Max Pressure:} Selects phase maximising
        queue pressure. Vehicle-centric; no pedestrian safety.
      \item \textbf{SOTL:} Switches when red-side queue
        $\Phi_\text{red}(t)\!\geq\!\kappa\!=\!5$ and $\tau\!\geq\!\tau_\text{min}$.
      \item \textbf{DQN-AR:} DQN with acceptance-rejection safety filter.
        Zero violations; $\mathcal{O}(1)$ per-step cost.
      \item \textbf{SPRe+ :} Projects policy onto feasible simplex
        via QP. $\mathcal{O}(|\mathcal{A}|^2)$; equivalent to DQN-AR here.
      \item \textbf{Action-Constrained PPO (proposed):} Masked softmax over
        $\mathcal{C}(s)$. Learns safe behaviour during training.
    \end{itemize}
  \end{block}

\heading{Key Findings}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{High Efficiency}: PPO reduces vehicle wait times by \textbf{8.46\%} (1$\times$1) and \textbf{4.38\%} (3$\times$3) compared to DQN-AR.
  \item \textbf{Guaranteed Safety}: Learned agents achieve \textbf{0\% violations}. In contrast, rule-based heuristics violate safety rules in \textbf{19--71\%} of steps (e.g., Max Pressure at 53.5\%).
  \item \textbf{Network Scalability}: In the partially-controlled 3$\times$3 grid, PPO maintains a strong lead over Fixed-Time, narrowing the gap to Max Pressure ($-$6.4\%).
\end{itemize}

% Comprehensive Conclusions

\begin{block}{Conclusions \& Key Contributions}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Addressing Benchmark Limitations}: 
      While traditional heuristics like \textit{Max Pressure} and \textit{SOTL} are vehicle-centric and lack pedestrian safety, and state-of-the-art \textit{SPRe+} guarantees zero violations but suffers from high computation cost (QP projection overhead), our approach bridges these gaps.
      
    \item \textbf{Optimal Safety--Efficiency Trade-off}: 
      The proposed \textbf{Action-constrained PPO} achieves the lowest waiting time among safe agents with zero pedestrian-safety violations guaranteed by construction.
      
    \item \textbf{Eliminated Computational Overhead}: 
      By enforcing feasible-action masking during training, our method completely eliminates the post-hoc projection overhead of SPRe+ and the resampling loops of DQN-AR, ensuring scalability for larger networks.
      
    \item \textbf{Scalability \& Future Horizons}: 
      Longer training is expected to further widen the performance gap in complex $3\times3$ grids. Future steps include full 9-intersection multi-agent control and evaluation on real-world empirical data.
  \end{itemize}
\end{block}
